# NIS2 Directive — Network and Information Security

## What it is
NIS2 (Directive EU 2022/2555) replaced NIS1 in January 2023. Member states had until October 2024 to transpose into national law. It significantly expands scope and strengthens requirements compared to NIS1.

## Who it applies to
Two categories — Essential Entities and Important Entities — across 18 sectors including energy, transport, banking, health, digital infrastructure, ICT service management, public administration, and space. Size threshold: medium enterprises (50+ employees or €10M+ turnover) in covered sectors.

## Core obligations
- Risk management measures — technical and organisational measures proportionate to risk
- Incident reporting — significant incidents reported to national CSIRT within 24 hours (early warning), full report within 72 hours
- Business continuity — backup management, disaster recovery, crisis management
- Supply chain security — security in supplier and service provider relationships
- Vulnerability handling — coordinated vulnerability disclosure
- Use of cryptography and encryption
- Access control and asset management
- Multi-factor authentication and secure communications

## Significant incident definition
An incident that causes or can cause severe operational disruption or financial loss, or affects other persons by causing considerable material or non-material damage.

## Management accountability
NIS2 explicitly holds management bodies accountable. Management must approve security measures, oversee implementation, and can be held personally liable. Mandatory security training for management.

## Penalties
Essential entities: up to €10M or 2% of global annual turnover (whichever higher).
Important entities: up to €7M or 1.4% of global annual turnover.

## Relationship to other frameworks
NIS2 aligns with ISO 27001 — organisations certified to ISO 27001 are well positioned. ENISA provides implementation guidance. National competent authorities supervise compliance.
